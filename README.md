1. Interactive Learning Structure

The content is divided into 6 distinct views, accessible via a top navigation bar:

Foundations: Definitions & "Transformation vs. Digitization" card flip exercise.

Drivers & Data: Market pressure visualization & interactive scenario quizzes.

AI Landscape: Radar charts comparing Human EQ vs. AI IQ.

Harari's Pivot: The shift from Tools to Agents & the "Hacking Humans" calculator.

Agentic Future: Animated visualizations of the Agentic Loop (Perception ‚Üí Action).

Future Proofing: Governance frameworks (EU AI Act) & Career Sorting exercises.

2. Embedded Exercises

To ensure active learning, the lecture includes:

"Hacking Humans" Simulator: Adjust Biological Knowledge and Computing Power sliders to see the manipulation risk score.

Agentic Loop Animation: A click-through explanation of how autonomous agents "think."

Career Sorter: A decision game where students categorize tasks as "Agent-Suitable" or "Human-Essential."

Alignment Trap: A critical thinking scenario about AI goal optimization vs. ethics.

3. Technical Stack

Zero Dependencies: Runs entirely in the browser. No server required.

Tailwind CSS (CDN): For modern, responsive, and clean UI styling.

Chart.js (CDN): For dynamic data visualizations (Radar charts, Doughnut charts).

Vanilla JavaScript: Handles SPA routing, password logic, and interactive state.
